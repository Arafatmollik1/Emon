About

The four-year Asia Regional Resilience to a Changing Climate (ARRCC) programme that commenced in September 2018 aims to strengthen provision and uptake of weather and climate services across the South Asia Region. Whilst regional in nature, ARRCC also has a national focus on Bangladesh, Pakistan, Nepal and Afghanistan.

ARRCC focuses across all meteorological timescales (weather, seasonal, climate), and is being implemented in South Asia region with the main objective to build climate and environmental resilience by improving the application and access to weather and climate services at regional to national levels. In addition, the program is supporting the development of new technologies and innovative approaches to help vulnerable communities use weather warnings and forecasts to better prepare for climate-related shocks.

As part of ARRCC the Strengthening Climate Information Partnership in South Asia (SCIPSA) work package, SESAME+ an AgroMet decision support system is being developed by Regional Integrated Multi-Hazard Early Warning System (RIMES) and UK Met Office (UKMO) in collaboration with Bangladesh Meteorological Department (BMD).
